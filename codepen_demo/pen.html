WideText: simple responsive font sizing for one of more lines of text

To see WideText at work, resize your window and watch that ^ heading. To really see the magic, make your window narrow until it compresses the white boxes.

The max-width, outlines, and visible background are just to make it clear what's happening in this demo - in the wild, this would just look like text (comment out or remove any of the "just for demo purposes" styles to test).

An example of how several widetext svgs in a single container can be used for interesting typography

Remember that the background color and outlines are only for demonstation purposes - in practice you'd probably just style this to match the rest of your text. This example also demonstrates the ascender line to descender line sizing of the svg: it will necessarily be big enough to hold the tallest and longest possible characters.
